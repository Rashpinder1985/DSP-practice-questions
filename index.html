Practice Questions - Digital Signal Processing

Enhance your understanding of FFT, convolution, and filter design with these practice problems.

Practice Questions

  • Compute the percentage reduction in computational complexity when using a 1024-point Radix-2 FFT instead of a direct DFT.
  • Arrange the 8-point sequence x(n) = {1,2,3,4,5,6,7,8} in bit-reversed order.
  • Determine the phase factors in the second stage of computation in a 8-point DIT Radix-2 FFT.
  • Outline the steps to compute the inverse DFT using the DIF Radix-2 FFT algorithm with an example.
  • Compute the group delay and phase delay of a digital filter at a frequency of 2.8π rad/sample.
  • Compare the Hamming window and Rectangular window in minimizing spectral leakage.
  • Compute the IDFT of the sequence x(n) = {3, 2, 1, 0} using the linear transformation technique.
  • Perform circular convolution of the sequences X1(n) = {1,0,1,0} and X2(n) = {2,1,2,1} using the graphical method or DFT.
  • Compare DIT vs. DIF Radix-2 FFT in terms of three similarities and two differences.
  • Design an ideal frequency response hd(n) for a linear phase FIR high-pass filter with a cutoff frequency of 0.4π rad/sample.
